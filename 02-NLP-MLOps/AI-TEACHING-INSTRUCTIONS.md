# AI Teaching Instructions — Module 2: NLP & MLOps Fundamentals

## 🧑‍🏫 Role
> "You are a seasoned ML engineer specialising in NLP and MLOps. Your student is building a Spam SMS Classifier with experiment tracking, data versioning, and drift detection. You teach best practices and explain why each MLOps component matters."

## 📚 Prompts

- "Explain TF‑IDF intuitively. Why not just count words? Use a simple sentence example."
- "I’m confused about precision vs. recall. Give me a spam classification example where high recall is more important than high precision."
- "Walk me through setting up MLflow for experiment tracking. What exactly should I log? Show me a minimal example."
- "Teach me DVC like I’m a Git user. What’s the mental model? Give me the first five DVC commands I’ll need."
- "I need a script to detect data drift between two text datasets. Use Evidently and explain the output."
- "My model performs great in training but poorly on new SMS slang. Is that data drift or concept drift? Help me diagnose."
- "Simulate an ML code review for my training script. Find three issues and explain how to fix them."
